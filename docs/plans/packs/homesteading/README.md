# Pack: Homesteading

Agriculture, construction, trades, animal husbandry, and textile arts for self-sufficient living.

## Audience

Homesteaders, farmers, builders, and anyone pursuing self-sufficiency.

## Status

Idea -- collecting sources

---

## Sub-Topics

### Agriculture and Gardening

Soil science, composting, crop rotation, seed saving, pest management, season extension (greenhouses, cold frames), irrigation. Both annual vegetable gardening and perennial food systems.

### Permaculture and Agroforestry

Permaculture design principles, food forests, swales, keyline design, guild planting, zone planning. Long-term land management for food production.

### Animal Husbandry

Poultry (chickens, ducks, geese), goats, sheep, cattle, pigs, rabbits, bees. Breeding, feeding, health, housing, slaughter, and processing.

### Food Preservation

Canning (water bath, pressure), fermentation (sauerkraut, kimchi, lacto-fermentation), dehydration, smoking, salt-curing, root cellaring, freezing. Safe preservation methods and recipes.

### Construction and Natural Building

Timber framing, log building, cob, straw bale, earthbag, rammed earth, stone masonry. Foundation types, roofing, insulation. Practical shelter construction with local materials.

### Carpentry and Woodworking

Joinery, hand tool techniques, lumber milling, furniture building, tool maintenance. Emphasis on hand tools and low-power techniques for off-grid contexts.

### Plumbing and Water Systems

Gravity-fed water systems, well drilling and hand pumps, rainwater harvesting, greywater systems, basic plumbing (pipe fitting, soldering), composting toilets, septing systems.

### Electrical and Off-Grid Power

Solar PV system design, battery banks, charge controllers, micro-hydro, small wind turbines, basic household wiring, generator maintenance. Sizing and installation guides.

### Textile Arts

Spinning (wool, flax, cotton), weaving, knitting, sewing, dyeing, leather tanning and working. Clothing and fabric production from raw fiber.

### Metalworking and Blacksmithing

Forge setup, basic blacksmithing (tongs, hooks, hardware), tool making, welding basics, knife making. Repair and fabrication with simple equipment.

## Tiering Notes

- **Core** (~8-10 GB): Gardening SE, DIY SE, Appropedia, Practical Action, CD3WD, Energypedia, Low-tech Magazine (all already in recipes, ~7.5 GB). Add ATTRA guides, FAO key manuals, solar PV guides, water/well guides, beekeeping basics, Humanure Handbook.
- **Extended** (~15-22 GB): Add Cooking SE, My Self Reliance, iFixit, LibreTexts Engineering + Geosciences. Add Open Source Ecology, extension service publications, natural building references, woodworking/metalworking guides.
- **Reference** (~25-35 GB): Add deeper FAO library, specialty textile arts references, larger archival collections.

The existing recipe collection already covers a surprising amount of homesteading ground. Appropedia, Practical Action, CD3WD, and Low-tech Magazine together form a strong appropriate-technology core. The main gaps are in animal husbandry, textile arts, and metalworking.

## Relationship to Existing Content

This pack has the most overlap with existing Svalbard recipes of any proposed pack. Much of the core is already present:

- **Appropriate technology trio**: `appropedia` + `practical-action` + `cd3wd` covers water, energy, agriculture, and construction from a developing-world/appropriate-tech angle.
- **Energy**: `energypedia` + `lowtech-magazine` cover renewable energy and low-tech solutions.
- **Gardening/DIY**: `stackexchange-gardening` + `stackexchange-diy` provide deep Q&A.
- **Food**: `based-cooking` + `grimgrains` + `stackexchange-cooking` + `usda-nutrition` + `zimgit-food-preparation` cover cooking and nutrition.
- **Construction video**: `lrn-self-reliance` covers log cabin building.

The pack's contribution is formalizing this content into a coherent homesteading bundle and identifying the specific gaps (animal husbandry, textile arts, metalworking, permaculture design, seed saving) that need new sources.

## Open Questions

- Is there a good openly licensed permaculture design reference? The Mollison/Holmgren books are copyrighted. Appropedia has some permaculture content but it is fragmented.
- Should animal husbandry guides be sourced from FAO (developing-world focus) or from US extension services (temperate-climate focus), or both?
- How to handle the FAO download problem? Their publications site has no bulk download endpoint. Options: curate a specific list of key PDFs and fetch them individually, or scrape with zimit and filter.
- Should this pack include construction codes or standards (e.g., IRC residential building code)? These are copyrighted but some jurisdictions publish them openly.
- Is there an openly licensed equivalent to Storey's Country Wisdom guides for animal husbandry basics?
- Should renewable energy content (solar, wind, micro-hydro) live here, in a separate energy pack, or in both? Currently Energypedia is the strongest source and fits both contexts.
- The textile arts sub-topic is thin on sources. Are there open guilds, universities, or organizations publishing spinning/weaving/sewing references under permissive licenses?
